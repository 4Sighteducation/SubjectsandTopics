9. When studying classical thought learners will be required to show knowledge and understanding of:
9.1 the development of thought and ideas; how and why they emerged and how this was influenced by their broader cultural context
9.2 the influence of thought and ideas on the social, political, religious and cultural context of the classical world
9.3 ways in which these ideas and concepts can be interpreted, both in an ancient and modern context

10. When studying classical thought learners should be able to:
10.1 respond critically to the ideas and concepts studied, considering how they reflect the social, historical, political, religious and cultural context
10.2 apply their knowledge of cultural contexts to support, substantiate and inform their judgements
10.3 use their understanding of classical ideas and thinkers to demonstrate an understanding of the social, historical, and cultural context of the classical world
10.4 critically explore and explain the possibility of different interpretations of ideas from different audiences, from the classical period, up until the modern day

12. When studying literature learners will be required to show knowledge and understanding of:
12.1 ways in which writers shape meanings in classical texts
12.2 ways in which classical texts might be interpreted by different readers or audiences both in an ancient and modern context
12.3 ways in which classical texts relate to the historical, social, political, religious and cultural contexts in which they are written and received
12.4 ways in which classical texts relate to literary traditions and genres of the classical world

13. When studying literature learners should be able to:
13.1 respond critically to texts and consider how the attitudes and values of the classical world or author are expressed
13.2 apply their knowledge of cultural contexts to support, substantiate and inform evidence-based judgements about the classical texts
13.3 use classical texts to demonstrate an understanding of the social, historical and cultural context of the classical world, with recognition, where appropriate, of the complex issues of reliability and the difference between what a text might say and what can be inferred from this
13.4 critically explore and explain the possibility of different responses to a text from different audiences, from the classical period, up until the modern day
