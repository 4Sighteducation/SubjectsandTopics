1. Socio-cultural influences  
1.1. Physical activity and sport in the UK  
1.1.1. be familiar with current trends in participation in physical activity and sport:  
1.1.1.1. using different sources (such as Sport England, National Governing Bodies (NGBs) and Department of Culture, Media and Sport (DCMS))  
1.1.1.2. of different social groups  
1.1.1.3. in different physical activities and sports.  
1.2. Participation in physical activity and sport  
1.2.1. understand how different factors can affect participation, including:  
1.2.1.1. age  
1.2.1.2. gender  
1.2.1.3. ethnicity  
1.2.1.4. religion/culture  
1.2.1.5. family  
1.2.1.6. education  
1.2.1.7. time/work commitments  
1.2.1.8. cost/disposable income  
1.2.1.9. disability  
1.2.1.10. opportunity/access  
1.2.1.11. discrimination  
1.2.1.12. environment/climate  
1.2.1.13. media coverage  
1.2.1.14. role models  
1.2.2. understand strategies which can be used to improve participation:  
1.2.2.1. promotion  
1.2.2.2. provision  
1.2.2.3. access  
1.2.3. be able to apply examples from physical activity/sport to participation issues.  
1.3. Commercialisation of sport  
1.3.1. understand the influence of the media on the commercialisation of physical activity and sport:  
1.3.1.1. different types of media  
1.3.1.1.1. social  
1.3.1.1.2. internet  
1.3.1.1.3. TV/visual  
1.3.1.1.4. newspapers/magazines.  
1.3.2. know the meaning of commercialisation, including sport, sponsorship and the media (the golden triangle):  
1.3.2.1. positive and negative effects of the media on commercialisation  
1.3.2.2. be able to apply practical examples to these issues.  
1.3.3. understand the influence of sponsorship on the commercialisation of physical activity and sport:  
1.3.3.1. positive and negative effects of sponsorship on commercialisation  
1.3.3.2. be able to apply practical examples to the issue of sponsorship.  
1.4. Ethics in sport  
1.4.1. know and understand:  
1.4.1.1. the value of sportsmanship  
1.4.1.2. the reasons for gamesmanship and deviance in sport.  
1.4.2. be able to apply practical examples to these concepts.  
1.5. Drugs in sport  
1.5.1. know and understand the reasons why sports performers use drugs  
1.5.2. know the types of drugs and their effect on performance:  
1.5.2.1. anabolic steroids  
1.5.2.2. beta blockers  
1.5.2.3. stimulants  
1.5.3. give practical examples of the use of these drugs in sport.  
1.5.4. know and understand the impact of drug use in sport:  
1.5.4.1. on performers  
1.5.4.2. on sport itself.  
1.6. Violence in sport  
1.6.1. know and understand the reasons for player violence  
1.6.2. give practical examples of violence in sport.  

2. Sports psychology  
2.1. Characteristics of skilful movement  
2.1.1. know the definition of motor skills  
2.1.2. understand and be able to apply examples of the characteristics of skilful movement:  
2.1.2.1. efficiency  
2.1.2.2. pre-determined  
2.1.2.3. co-ordinated  
2.1.2.4. fluent  
2.1.2.5. aesthetic.  
2.2. Classification of skills  
2.2.1. know continua used in the classification of skills, including:  
2.2.1.1. simple to complex skills (difficulty continuum)  
2.2.1.2. open to closed skills (environmental continuum)  
2.2.2. be able to apply practical examples of skills for each continuum along with justification of their placement on both continua.  
2.3. Goal setting  
2.3.1. understand and be able to apply examples of the use of goal setting:  
2.3.1.1. for exercise/training adherence  
2.3.1.2. to motivate performers  
2.3.1.3. to improve and/or optimise performance  
2.3.2. understand the SMART principle of goal setting with practical examples (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Recorded, Timed)  
2.3.3. be able to apply the SMART principle to improve and/or optimise performance.  
2.4. Mental preparation  
2.4.1. know mental preparation techniques and be able to apply practical examples to their use:  
2.4.1.1. imagery  
2.4.1.2. mental rehearsal  
2.4.1.3. selective attention  
2.4.1.4. positive thinking.  
2.5. Types of guidance  
2.5.1. understand types of guidance, their advantages and disadvantages, and be able to apply practical examples to their use:  
2.5.1.1. visual  
2.5.1.2. verbal  
2.5.1.3. manual  
2.5.1.4. mechanical.  
2.6. Types of feedback  
2.6.1. understand types of feedback and be able to apply practical examples to their use:  
2.6.1.1. intrinsic  
2.6.1.2. extrinsic  
2.6.1.3. knowledge of performance  
2.6.1.4. knowledge of results  
2.6.1.5. positive  
2.6.1.6. negative.  

3. Health, fitness and well-being  
3.1. Health, fitness and well-being  
3.1.1. know what is meant by health, fitness and well-being  
3.1.2. understand the different health benefits of physical activity and consequences of a sedentary lifestyle:  
3.1.2.1. physical:  
3.1.2.1.1. injury  
3.1.2.1.2. coronary heart disease (CHD)  
3.1.2.1.3. blood pressure  
3.1.2.1.4. bone density  
3.1.2.1.5. obesity  
3.1.2.1.6. Type 2 diabetes  
3.1.2.1.7. posture  
3.1.2.1.8. fitness  
3.1.2.2. emotional:  
3.1.2.2.1. self-esteem/confidence  
3.1.2.2.2. stress management  
3.1.2.2.3. image  
3.1.2.3. social:  
3.1.2.3.1. friendship  
3.1.2.3.2. belonging to a group  
3.1.2.3.3. loneliness  
3.1.3. be able to apply the above to different age groups  
3.1.4. be able to respond to data about health, fitness and well-being  
3.2. Diet and nutrition  
3.2.1. know the definition of a balanced diet  
3.2.2. know the components of a balanced diet  
3.2.2.1. carbohydrates  
3.2.2.2. proteins  
3.2.2.3. fats  
3.2.2.4. minerals  
3.2.2.5. vitamins  
3.2.2.6. fibre  
3.2.2.7. water and hydration  
3.2.3. understand the effect of diet and hydration on energy use in physical activity  
3.2.4. be able to apply practical examples from physical activity and sport to diet and hydration.
