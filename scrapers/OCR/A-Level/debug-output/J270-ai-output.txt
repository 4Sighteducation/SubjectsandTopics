1. Citizenship Studies (J270)
1.1 Component 01: Citizenship in perspective (J270/01)
1.1.1 Rights, the law and the legal system in England and Wales
1.1.1.1 Rights and responsibilities
1.1.1.1.1 Know, understand and analyse the human, moral, legal and political rights, and the duties, equalities and freedoms of citizens.
1.1.1.1.2 Know the age of criminal responsibility and other legal ages when young people become legally responsible for their actions such as drive, marry, vote or join the armed forces.
1.1.1.1.3 Know the background to Magna Carta and its main provisions.
1.1.1.1.4 Know the main provisions of: the UN Universal Declaration on Human Rights; the European Convention on Human Rights; the United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child and the 1998 Human Rights Act.
1.1.1.1.5 Know and understand the right to representation including the role and history of trade unions and employee associations.
1.1.1.1.6 Analyse rights in local, national and global situations where there is conflict.
1.1.1.2 The law
1.1.1.2.1 Know the nature of rules and laws.
1.1.1.2.2 Understand the presumption of innocence and equality before the law.
1.1.1.2.3 Evaluate the effectiveness of at least one piece of legislation.
1.1.1.2.4 Know the key differences between criminal and civil law.
1.1.1.2.5 Know the main sources of law: common law, legislation and judge-made law (precedent).
1.1.1.2.6 Know different legal jurisdictions of the UK: Northern Ireland, Scotland, England and Wales.
1.1.1.3 The legal system (England and Wales)
1.1.1.3.1 Know and understand the operation of the justice system: the role and powers of the police; the role and powers of the judiciary; the role of legal representatives; the different types of criminal and civil courts and how they work; tribunals and other means of civil dispute resolution.
1.1.1.3.2 Know and understand about the operation of the youth justice system.
1.1.1.3.3 Know and understand the responsibilities and roles of citizens in the legal system as a juror, magistrate, special constable, police commissioner and member of a tribunal hearing.
1.1.1.3.4 Understand the factors affecting crime rates in society.
1.1.1.3.5 Know and understand the nature and purpose of different sanctions for different criminal offences.
1.1.2 Democracy and government
1.1.2.1 Democracy, elections and voting in the UK
1.1.2.1.1 Know and understand the concept of democracy.
1.1.2.1.2 Know the key features of democratic government including: regular and free elections; secret ballots; an inclusive franchise; a choice of candidates/political parties.
1.1.2.1.3 Know the difference between classical democracy and representative democracy.
1.1.2.1.4 Know that the UK is a representative democracy with a sovereign parliament and that MPs represent constituencies.
1.1.2.1.5 Know who can and cannot vote in UK parliamentary elections.
1.1.2.1.6 Know the different ways in which citizens can contribute to parliamentary democracy and hold those in power to account.
1.1.2.1.7 Know the major political parties operating in the UK.
1.1.2.1.8 Know the key features of electoral systems used within the UK including: first-past-the-post; proportional representation and referendum.
1.1.2.2 National, local, regional and devolved government
1.1.2.2.1 Know that political parties with large numbers of parliamentary seats have the potential to form a government alone or to do so through coalition with others.
1.1.2.2.2 Know and understand the key roles in UK Government – Prime Minister, Cabinet and ministers.
1.1.2.2.3 Know the role, structure and organisation of local, regional and devolved government in the UK.
1.1.2.2.4 Know and understand the changing relationships between England, Northern Ireland, Scotland and Wales.
1.1.2.3 British constitution
1.1.2.3.1 Know the separate but complementary roles of the executive, legislature, judiciary and the Monarchy in UK government and understand the differences between them.
1.1.2.3.2 Know the separate but complementary roles of the House of Commons, House of Lords and the Monarch within the bicameral Westminster parliament and the differences between them.
1.1.2.3.3 Know the main roles of front and back bench MPs, the Commons Speaker and the party whips.
1.1.2.3.4 Know that government administration is divided into departments, ministries and agencies.
1.1.2.4 The economy, finance and money
1.1.2.4.1 Know how public taxes are raised by local and national government.
1.1.2.4.2 Know the main areas of public expenditure for local and national government.
1.1.2.4.3 Analyse and evaluate different viewpoints on taxation.
1.1.2.5 The role of the media and free press
1.1.2.5.1 Know the legal rights and responsibilities of a ‘free press’.
1.1.2.5.2 Understand the media’s moral responsibility to report accurately and to respect people’s privacy and dignity.
1.1.2.5.3 Know how media regulation operates in the UK.
1.1.2.5.4 Analyse how the media is used by groups wishing to influence public opinion.
1.1.2.6 Citizenship participation in the UK
1.1.2.6.1 Know the role of citizens in the UK constitution.
1.1.2.6.2 Know the opportunities for citizen participation in UK democracy, including the National Citizen Service.
1.1.2.6.3 Know the different forms of action citizens can take to promote particular viewpoints and hold those in power to account including: joining an interest group; trade union or political party; standing for election; campaigning; lobbying; petitions and joining a demonstration.
1.1.2.6.4 Know the ways in which citizens can contribute to wider public life including: joining an interest group, advocacy and volunteering.
1.1.2.7 Politics beyond the UK
1.1.2.7.1 Know the key differences between the nature and extent of citizens’ political participation in the UK and one other democratic system.
1.1.2.7.2 Know the key differences between the nature and extent of citizens’ political participation in the UK and one non-democratic system.
1.1.3 The UK and the wider world
1.1.3.1 Identities and diversity in UK society
1.1.3.1.1 Know the main factors that affect people’s sense of identity at individual, group, national and global scales.
1.1.3.1.2 Know the constituent nations of the UK and understand how people’s connections with these nations impact on their sense of identity.
1.1.3.1.3 Know that, in UK society, people have a range of identities which are often diverse and complex.
1.1.3.1.4 Understand the reasons why people migrate from one place to another.
1.1.3.1.5 Know the broad patterns of migration into and out of the UK in the last 60 years.
1.1.3.1.6 Know what it means for people to seek asylum and understand their reasons for seeking asylum.
1.1.3.1.7 Understand why mutual respect and understanding, and community cohesion are important in a democratic society.
1.1.3.2 The UK and its relations with the wider world
1.1.3.2.1 Know that the UK is a member of international organisations including: the United Nations and its agencies; North Atlantic Treaty Organisation (NATO); the Council of Europe; the Commonwealth and the World Trade Organisation (WTO).
1.1.3.2.2 Know the principles underpinning the formation and development of the European Union.
1.1.3.2.3 Know the methods (including short- and long-term practical support, mediation, sanctions or force) available to governments that wish to intervene in international disagreements or conflicts, and to support other governments or peoples in crisis.
1.1.3.2.4 Understand why it is necessary to make international agreements to protect victims of conflict and to establish rules of warfare.
1.1.3.2.5 Analyse the role of one international non-governmental organisation in an international crisis, disagreement or conflict.

1.2.4 Citizenship Action
1.2.4.1 Working as a team, identify, research (using primary and secondary sources) and evaluate a citizenship issue and show how this issue links to the citizenship specification.
1.2.4.2 Describe and explain their own viewpoint on the issue, and those of other people, using the evidence they have gathered.
1.2.4.3 Using their primary and secondary research, and in collaboration with others, plan an appropriate Citizenship Action.
1.2.4.4 In collaboration with others, undertake an appropriate action that incorporates problem-solving and advocacy.
1.2.4.5 Critically evaluate the action as a whole, including what they felt went well and what they would do better in the future.
1.2.4.6 Know and understand how actions can impact on a local, national and global setting, and steps they could take to enlarge their action.
