1. Critical and Contextual Studies (J176)
   1.1. Component 01: Portfolio
      1.1.1. Learners should produce:
         1.1.1.1. A portfolio of practical work showing their personal response to either a centre- or learner-set starting point, brief, scenario or stimulus.
      1.1.2. Learners must show they have:
         1.1.2.1. Developed ideas through investigations and demonstrated critical understanding of sources from a given starting point.
         1.1.2.2. Produced material informed by context that is relevant to the development of their ideas.
         1.1.2.3. Refined work by exploring ideas, selecting and experimenting with media appropriate to their chosen specification title and area(s) of study.
         1.1.2.4. Recorded ideas, observations and insights relevant to intentions as work progresses.
         1.1.2.5. Presented a personal and meaningful response that realises intentions.
      1.1.3. The Portfolio is marked using the relevant marking criteria for the Portfolio.
   1.2. Component 02: Externally set task
      1.2.1. The early release paper will be issued on 2 January each year and will provide learners with five themes, each with a range of written and visual starting points and stimuli.
      1.2.2. Learners are expected to choose one option for which they will generate an appropriate personal response of one or more artefact(s)/product(s)/personal outcome(s) within a 10-hour supervised time period.
      1.2.3. Planning and preparation:
         1.2.3.1. Learners must be given a preparatory period during which they will research, plan and develop ideas for their own response to the option they have chosen.
         1.2.3.2. Guidance should be given to learners about availability and choice of materials, health and safety, avoidance of plagiarism and completion of work in accordance with specification requirements and procedures.
      1.2.4. Realising intentions:
         1.2.4.1. Learners will have up to 10 hours in which to independently realise their response(s) through the production of one or more artefact(s)/product(s)/personal outcome(s).
         1.2.4.2. The 10-hour supervised time period should be conducted under formal examination conditions.
      1.2.5. The Externally set task is marked using the relevant marking criteria for the Externally set task.
   1.3. Core content of GCSE (9–1) in Art and Design
      1.3.1. Scope and Context
         1.3.1.1. The specification is designed to encourage, enable and inspire learners to:
            1.3.1.1.1. Actively engage with the creative processes of art, craft and design in their chosen title(s) and area(s) of study.
            1.3.1.1.2. Develop, through the course of study, to be reflective thinkers with enquiring minds.
            1.3.1.1.3. Become effective and independent learners through the study of art, craft and design.
            1.3.1.1.4. Develop their creative, imaginative and intuitive capabilities when exploring, creating and producing their images, artefacts and products.
            1.3.1.1.5. Become confident in taking risks, learning from their experiences through exploring and experimenting with ideas, processes, materials, techniques, digital and non-digital media.
            1.3.1.1.6. Develop critical understanding through their chosen area(s) of study, using personal interpretation, considered selection of sources and independent judgements through an active investigation process.
            1.3.1.1.7. Develop and refine their ideas, supported by an understanding of their context and what has informed them.
            1.3.1.1.8. Work through purposeful engagement with an appropriate range of media, materials, techniques, processes and technologies with the purpose and intent to impact on the progress of work.
            1.3.1.1.9. Develop knowledge and understanding of art, craft and design in historical and contemporary contexts, societies and cultures appropriate to their chosen area(s) of study.
            1.3.1.1.10. Develop through the course of study, an awareness of the different work practices and roles of the creative and cultural industries in the production of art, craft and design.
            1.3.1.1.11. Show an understanding of the purpose, intentions and functions of art, craft and design, and how this impacts on their own work.
            1.3.1.1.12. Identify and acknowledge all sources used during their research which should be in the form of a bibliography of books, journals and websites.
            1.3.1.1.13. Demonstrate safe working practices in art, craft and design.
      1.3.2. Approaches
         1.3.2.1. Learners use a range of materials and techniques along with a variety of approaches to produce work.
         1.3.2.2. Learners may use any chosen digital and non-digital media or a combination of both.
         1.3.2.3. Learners use critical and contextual understanding to support practical work.
         1.3.2.4. Learners may take an applied approach when working to a brief or stimuli.
         1.3.2.5. There is a need for all learners to develop understanding and skills in forms of drawing that are appropriate to their chosen specification title and the context in which the drawing is undertaken.
         1.3.2.6. Drawing may take different forms. For example, it could be shown through the use of mark-making as a way of recording ideas, observations or insights. Other ways of drawing may include two- and three-dimensional designs, storyboarding, stitch and collage. It may be approached through traditional or digital media and emerging technologies, or a combination.
      1.3.3. Core Skills
         1.3.3.1. All learners engaging with these specifications must demonstrate the ability to:
            1.3.3.1.1. Develop their ideas through investigations informed by selecting and critically analysing sources.
            1.3.3.1.2. Apply an understanding of relevant practices in the creative and cultural industries to their work.
            1.3.3.1.3. Refine their ideas as work progresses through experimenting with media, materials, techniques and processes.
            1.3.3.1.4. Record their ideas, observations, insights and independent judgements, visually and through written annotation, using appropriate specialist vocabulary, as work progresses.
            1.3.3.1.5. Use visual language critically as appropriate to their own creative intentions and chosen area(s) of study through effective and safe use of media, materials, techniques, processes, technologies.
            1.3.3.1.6. Use drawing skills for different needs and purposes, appropriate to the context in which it is used.
            1.3.3.1.7. Realise personal intentions through the sustained application of the creative process.
      1.3.4. Core Knowledge and Understanding
         1.3.4.1. Learners are required to show knowledge and understanding through practical skills in the development of ideas. This may include:
            1.3.4.1.1. The work and approaches of artists, craftspeople or designers from contemporary and/or historical contexts, periods, societies and cultures.
            1.3.4.1.2. Contemporary and/or historical environments, situations or issues.
            1.3.4.1.3. Other relevant sources researched by the learner in the chosen qualification title and area(s) of study.
            1.3.4.1.4. The ways in which meanings, ideas and intentions can be communicated through visual, sensory and tactile language, using formal elements, including colour, line, form, tone, texture.
            1.3.4.1.5. The characteristics, properties and effects of using different media, materials, techniques and processes, and the ways in which they can be used in relation to learners’ own creative intentions and chosen area(s) of study.
            1.3.4.1.6. The different purposes, intentions and functions of art, craft and design in a variety of contexts and as appropriate to learners’ own work.
   1.4. Content of Art and Design: Critical and Contextual Studies (J176)
      1.4.1. Overview
         1.4.1.1. Critical and Contextual Studies is defined here as the critical analysis, interpretation and reflective appraisal from a contemporary perspective of the work of artists, craftspeople and designers.
         1.4.1.2. Learners must explore, acquire and develop skills, knowledge and understanding through the application of research and analysis in the context of critical and contextual understanding.
         1.4.1.3. Learners must explore in written, practical or a combination of both approaches, relevant critical and contextual sources such as the work of historical and contemporary practitioners and the different purposes, intentions and functions of in art, craft or design as appropriate to their own area of investigation.
         1.4.1.4. Learners must demonstrate the ability to analyse critically, and interpret the work of artists, craftspeople and designers taking into account the context (e.g. historical, cultural, social, economic or political) of their production in order to understand meanings, purposes, relationships and influences.
      1.4.2. Areas of Study
         1.4.2.1. Learners are required to work in one or more area(s) of Critical and Contextual Studies, such as those listed below. Combinations of these areas are also possible:
            1.4.2.1.1. Artists, craftspeople and designers
            1.4.2.1.2. Genres
            1.4.2.1.3. Movements
            1.4.2.1.4. Themes, concepts and ideas in art, craft and design
            1.4.2.1.5. Contemporary art, craft and design
            1.4.2.1.6. Popular culture
            1.4.2.1.7. The human form
            1.4.2.1.8. Still life
            1.4.2.1.9. Designed objects
            1.4.2.1.10. Landscape
            1.4.2.1.11. Natural forms
      1.4.3. Techniques
         1.4.3.1. Learners must demonstrate the ability to analyse critically, and interpret the work of artists, craftspeople and designers, taking into account the context (e.g. historical, cultural, social, economic, political) of their production in order to understand meanings, purposes, relationships and influences. Learners must choose appropriate methods and media to communicate their responses and to demonstrate their knowledge and understanding of their chosen area(s) of study.
      1.4.4. Skills
         1.4.4.1. Learners are required to demonstrate the knowledge, skills and understanding as defined in the Art and Design Core Content section of this specification in the context of their chosen area(s) of Critical and Contextual Studies.
         1.4.4.2. In addition, learners will be required to demonstrate skills in the following:
            1.4.4.2.1. Develop their ideas through investigations informed by selecting and critically analysing Critical and Contextual sources.
            1.4.4.2.2. Apply an understanding of relevant creative and cultural practices in relation to Critical and Contextual Studies.
            1.4.4.2.3. Refine their ideas as work progresses through researching, selecting, analysing and presenting artefact(s)/product(s)/personal outcome(s).
            1.4.4.2.4. Record their ideas, observations, insights and independent judgements, in ways that are appropriate to the Critical and Contextual Studies title, such as selecting and interpreting research material and analysing materials and techniques used by artists or designers.
            1.4.4.2.5. Use appropriate specialist vocabulary through either visual communication or written annotation, or both, within Critical and Contextual Studies.
            1.4.4.2.6. Use visual language critically as appropriate to their own creative intentions in Critical and Contextual Studies and chosen area(s) of study through effective and safe use of media, materials, techniques, processes, technologies.
            1.4.4.2.7. Use drawing skills for different needs and purposes, appropriate to the context used. Drawing may take the form of illustrated journals, study sheets and transpositions using appropriate media and materials.
            1.4.4.2.8. Realise personal intentions in Critical and Contextual Studies through the sustained application of the creative process.
      1.4.5. Knowledge and Understanding
         1.4.5.1. Learners are required to demonstrate the knowledge and understanding listed below through practical application of skills to realise personal intentions relevant to their chosen area(s) of Critical and Contextual Studies.
         1.4.5.2. Learners are required to know and understand how sources inspire the development of their ideas.
         1.4.5.3. Reference should be made to the following:
            1.4.5.3.1. The work and approaches of artists, craftspeople or designers from contemporary and/or historical contexts, periods, societies and cultures.
            1.4.5.3.2. Contemporary and/or historical environments, situations or issues.
            1.4.5.3.3. Other relevant sources researched by the learner in their chosen area(s) of critical and contextual studies.
            1.4.5.3.4. The ways in which meanings, ideas and intentions can be communicated in Critical and Contextual Studies through visual and tactile language, using formal elements, including colour, line, form, tone, texture.
            1.4.5.3.5. The characteristics, properties and effects of using different media, materials, techniques and processes, and the ways in which they can be used in relation to learners’ own creative intentions and chosen area(s) of Critical and Contextual Studies.
            1.4.5.3.6. The different purposes, intentions and functions of critical and contextual studies in a variety of contexts and as appropriate to learners’ own work.
