1 Overview
1.1 Learners are required to choose one or more area(s) of study:
1.1.1 Advertising
1.1.2 Communication graphics
1.1.3 Design for print
1.1.4 Illustration
1.1.5 Interactive design (including web, app and game design)
1.1.6 Multi-media
1.1.7 Package design
1.1.8 Signage
1.1.9 Typography

2 Core Knowledge and Understanding
2.1 Learners are required to show knowledge and understanding through practical skills in the development of ideas. This may include:
2.1.1 The work and approaches of artists, craftspeople or designers from contemporary and/or historical contexts, periods, societies and cultures
2.1.2 Contemporary and/or historical environments, situations or issues
2.1.3 Other relevant sources researched by the learner in the chosen qualification title and area(s) of study
2.1.4 The ways in which meanings, ideas and intentions can be communicated through visual, sensory and tactile language, using formal elements, including:
2.1.4.1 Colour
2.1.4.2 Line
2.1.4.3 Form
2.1.4.4 Tone
2.1.4.5 Texture
2.1.5 The characteristics, properties and effects of using different media, materials, techniques and processes, and the ways in which they can be used in relation to learners’ own creative intentions and chosen area(s) of study
2.1.6 The different purposes, intentions and functions of art, craft and design in a variety of contexts and as appropriate to learners’ own work
