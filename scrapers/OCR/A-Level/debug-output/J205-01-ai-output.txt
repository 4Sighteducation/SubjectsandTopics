1. Introduction to Economics
1.1. Main economic groups and factors of production
1.1.1. Explain the role of the main economic groups: consumers, producers, and the government, including their interdependence.
1.1.2. Explain the factors of production: land, labour, capital, and enterprise, including how they might be combined.
1.2. The basic economic problem
1.2.1. Explain what is meant by scarce resources and unlimited wants.
1.2.2. Explain the economic problem, including the questions of how resources should be allocated, what, for whom, and how goods and services should be produced.
1.2.3. Explain what is meant by opportunity cost.
1.2.4. Evaluate the costs and benefits of economic choices, including the impact on economic, social, and environmental sustainability.

2. The role of markets and money
2.1. The role of markets
2.1.1. Explain what is meant by a market.
2.1.2. Explain the features of the primary, secondary, and tertiary sectors, including the difference between the production of goods and services.
2.1.3. Explain the difference between factor and product markets, including their interdependence.
2.1.4. Evaluate the costs and benefits of specialisation and exchange in markets, including for producers, workers, regions, and countries.
2.2. Demand
2.2.1. Explain what is meant by demand.
2.2.2. Draw and explain a demand curve using data, including individual and market demand.
2.2.3. Draw shifts of, and movements along, the demand curve.
2.2.4. Analyse the causes and consequences for consumers and producers, of shifts of, and movements along, the demand curve.
2.2.5. Explain price elasticity of demand.
2.2.6. Draw demand curves of different elasticity.
2.2.7. Evaluate the importance of price elasticity of demand for consumers and producers.
2.3. Supply
2.3.1. Explain what is meant by supply.
2.3.2. Draw and explain a supply curve using data, including individual and market supply.
2.3.3. Draw shifts of, and movements along, the supply curve.
2.3.4. Analyse the causes and consequences for consumers and producers, of shifts of, and movements along, the supply curve.
2.3.5. Explain price elasticity of supply.
2.3.6. Draw supply curves of different elasticity.
2.3.7. Evaluate the importance of price elasticity of supply for consumers and producers.
2.4. Price
2.4.1. Explain price as a reflection of worth and its role in determining an efficient distribution of resources.
2.4.2. Explain what is meant by equilibrium price and quantity.
2.4.3. Draw and analyse the interaction of demand and supply.
2.4.4. Explain the role of markets in the determination of price and the allocation of resources.
2.4.5. Analyse how the market forces of demand and supply affect equilibrium price and quantity.
2.5. Competition
2.5.1. Explain competition between producers in a market economy, including the reasons why producers compete.
2.5.2. Analyse how competition affects price.
2.5.3. Evaluate the economic impact of competition on producers and consumers.
2.5.4. Explain the meaning of monopoly and oligopoly and how they differ from competitive markets.
2.6. Production
2.6.1. Explain the role of producers, including individuals, firms, and the government.
2.6.2. Evaluate the importance of production and productivity for the economy.
2.6.3. Calculate and explain total cost, average cost, total revenue, average revenue, profit, and loss.
2.6.4. Evaluate the importance of cost, revenue, profit, and loss for producers, including how costs and revenues affect profit and supply.
2.6.5. Explain what is meant by economies of scale.
2.7. The labour market
2.7.1. Explain the role and operation of the labour market, including the interaction between workers and employers.
2.7.2. Analyse the determination of wages through supply and demand, including factors affecting the supply and demand of labour.
2.7.3. Explain and calculate gross and net pay, including deductions through income tax, national insurance, and pension contributions.
2.8. The role of money and financial markets
2.8.1. Explain the role of money as a medium of exchange.
2.8.2. Explain the role of the financial sector for the economy, including financial institutions such as banks, building societies, and insurance companies.
2.8.3. Evaluate the importance of the financial sector for consumers, producers, and government.
2.8.4. Analyse how different interest rates affect the levels of saving, borrowing, and investment.
2.8.5. Calculate the effect on savings and borrowings of changes in the rate of interest.
