6. When studying literature learners will be required to show knowledge and understanding of:
6.1 ways in which writers shape meanings in classical texts
6.2 ways in which classical texts might be interpreted by different readers or audiences both in an ancient and modern context
6.3 ways in which classical texts relate to the historical, social, political, religious and cultural contexts in which they are written and received
6.4 ways in which classical texts relate to literary traditions and genres of the classical world

7. When studying literature learners should be able to:
7.1 respond critically to texts and consider how the attitudes and values of the classical world or author are expressed
7.2 apply their knowledge of cultural contexts to support, substantiate and inform evidence-based judgements about the classical texts
7.3 use classical texts to demonstrate an understanding of the social, historical and cultural context of the classical world, with recognition, where appropriate, of the complex issues of reliability and the difference between what a text might say and what can be inferred from this
7.4 critically explore and explain the possibility of different responses to a text from different audiences, from the classical period, up until the modern day
