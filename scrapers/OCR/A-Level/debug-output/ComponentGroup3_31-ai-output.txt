9. When studying classical thought learners will be required to show knowledge and understanding of:
9.1 the development of thought and ideas; how and why they emerged and how this was influenced by their broader cultural context
9.2 the influence of thought and ideas on the social, political, religious and cultural context of the classical world
9.3 ways in which these ideas and concepts can be interpreted, both in an ancient and modern context

10. When studying classical thought learners should be able to:
10.1 respond critically to the ideas and concepts studied, considering how they reflect the social, historical, political, religious and cultural context
10.2 apply their knowledge of cultural contexts to support, substantiate and inform their judgements
10.3 use their understanding of classical ideas and thinkers to demonstrate an understanding of the social, historical, and cultural context of the classical world
10.4 critically explore and explain the possibility of different interpretations of ideas from different audiences, from the classical period, up until the modern day

12. When studying visual/material culture sources learners will be required to show knowledge and understanding of:
12.1 the appearance, style, content and original location (as applicable) of the sources
12.2 what the sources can tell us about the classical world, and what they cannot
12.3 the ways in which the social, political, religious and cultural context of production impacts on the creation of visual/material culture
12.4 appropriate methods of analysis and interpretation, including issues of purpose, production and form
12.5 the range of possible interpretations of visual/material culture when looked at from different perspectives, in an ancient and modern context

13. When studying visual/material culture learners should be able to:
13.1 respond critically to artefacts, identifying different possible interpretations, taking account of issues such as those of audience and purpose
13.2 know and understand how materials and artefacts relate to their wider context and how this social, historical, political, religious and cultural context affected their creation and interpretation
13.3 evaluate the usefulness of visual/material culture when investigating the classical world
13.4 apply their knowledge of cultural contexts to support, substantiate and inform their judgements
13.5 use their understanding of visual/material culture to demonstrate an understanding of the social, historical and cultural context of the classical world with recognition, of the limitations of evidence (such as the issue of fragmentary or relocated material)
13.6 critically explore and explain the possibility of different responses to materials from different audiences, from the classical period, up until the modern day
