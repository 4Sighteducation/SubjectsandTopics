1. Subject: Music (J536)
   1.1. Components
      1.1.1. Component 01: Integrated portfolio
         1.1.1.1. Performance on the learner’s chosen instrument
         1.1.1.2. Composition to a brief set by the learner
      1.1.2. Component 02: Integrated portfolio (Moderated postal)
         1.1.2.1. Performance on the learner’s chosen instrument
         1.1.2.2. Composition to a brief set by the learner
      1.1.3. Component 03: Practical component
         1.1.3.1. Ensemble performance
         1.1.3.2. Composition to an OCR set brief
      1.1.4. Component 04: Practical component (Moderated postal)
         1.1.4.1. Ensemble performance
         1.1.4.2. Composition to an OCR set brief
      1.1.5. Component 05: Listening and appraising
         1.1.5.1. A written paper, with audio recording
         1.1.5.2. Aural recognition and context of unheard/unfamiliar music from within the Areas of Study 2, 3, 4 & 5
   1.2. Sections within components
      1.2.1. Content of OCR GCSE (9–1) in Music (J536)
         1.2.1.1. Musical elements
            1.2.1.1.1. Organisation of pitch
            1.2.1.1.2. Tonality
            1.2.1.1.3. Structure
            1.2.1.1.4. Sonority
            1.2.1.1.5. Texture
            1.2.1.1.6. Tempo, metre and rhythm
            1.2.1.1.7. Dynamics
         1.2.1.2. Musical contexts
            1.2.1.2.1. Effect of purpose and intention
            1.2.1.2.2. Effect of audience, time and place
         1.2.1.3. Musical language
            1.2.1.3.1. Reading and writing of staff notation
            1.2.1.3.2. Major and minor chords
            1.2.1.3.3. Recognising and using appropriate musical vocabulary
         1.2.1.4. Perform
            1.2.1.4.1. Use of musical elements, techniques and resources
            1.2.1.4.2. Perform music with control
         1.2.1.5. Compose
            1.2.1.5.1. Use of musical elements, techniques and resources
            1.2.1.5.2. Compose music that develops musical ideas
         1.2.1.6. Appraise
            1.2.1.6.1. Analyse and evaluate music
            1.2.1.6.2. Use knowledge and understanding of musical elements
      1.2.2. Areas of Study
         1.2.2.1. Area of Study 1: My Music
            1.2.2.1.1. Study their instrument
            1.2.2.1.2. Capabilities and limitations of their instrument
            1.2.2.1.3. Perform one or more piece(s) for their instrument
            1.2.2.1.4. Compose a piece of their choice
         1.2.2.2. Area of Study 2: The Concerto Through Time
            1.2.2.2.1. Study The Concerto and its development from 1650 to 1910
            1.2.2.2.2. Understand the characteristics of Baroque, Classical and Romantic music
            1.2.2.2.3. Study and understand how composers use musical elements and compositional devices
         1.2.2.3. Area of Study 3: Rhythms of the World
            1.2.2.3.1. Study traditional rhythmic roots from four geographical regions
            1.2.2.3.2. Understand characteristic rhythmic features
            1.2.2.3.3. Study and understand how a range of musical elements is combined in traditional music
         1.2.2.4. Area of Study 4: Film Music
            1.2.2.4.1. Study a range of music used for films
            1.2.2.4.2. Study how composers create music to support, express, complement and enhance
            1.2.2.4.3. Study and understand how composers use music dramatically and expressively
         1.2.2.5. Area of Study 5: Conventions of Pop
            1.2.2.5.1. Study a range of popular music from the 1950s to the present day
            1.2.2.5.2. Study and demonstrate an understanding of vocal and instrumental techniques
            1.2.2.5.3. Study and understand how composers of popular music use and develop musical elements
      1.2.3. Content of Integrated portfolio (01 moderated upload or 02 moderated postal)
         1.2.3.1. Develop an understanding of performance and composition
         1.2.3.2. Develop playing skills and abilities through regular practice
         1.2.3.3. Demonstrate knowledge of composition techniques
      1.2.4. Content of Practical component (03 moderated upload or 04 moderated postal)
         1.2.4.1. Further develop skills and understanding of performance and composition
         1.2.4.2. Focus on the demands of performing within an ensemble
         1.2.4.3. Compose a piece of music in response to a brief set by OCR
      1.2.5. Content of Listening and appraising (05)
         1.2.5.1. Assess knowledge and understanding of the content of Area of Study 2, 3, 4, and 5
         1.2.5.2. Demonstrate and apply knowledge of musical elements, contexts, and language
         1.2.5.3. Use appraising skills to make evaluative and critical judgements
      1.2.6. Prior knowledge, learning and progression
         1.2.6.1. No prior knowledge or learning of the subject is required
         1.2.6.2. A background in music would be beneficial
         1.2.6.3. Progression to further study from GCSE will depend upon the number and nature of the grades achieved
