1. Geographical Skills
1.1. Cartographic Skills
1.1.1. Select, adapt and construct maps, using appropriate scales and annotations, to present information.
1.1.2. Interpret cross-sections and transects.
1.1.3. Use and understand coordinates, scale and distance.
1.1.4. Extract, interpret, analyse and evaluate information.
1.1.5. Use and understand gradient, contour and spot height (on OS and other isoline maps).
1.1.6. Describe, interpret and analyse geo-spatial data presented in a GIS framework.
1.1.7. Maps to be studied: Atlas maps, OS maps (1:50 000 and 1:25 000 scales), Base maps, Choropleth maps, Isoline maps, Flow line maps, Desire-line maps, Sphere of influence maps, Thematic maps, Route maps, Sketch maps.

1.2. Graphical Skills
1.2.1. Select, adapt and construct appropriate graphs and charts, using appropriate scales and annotations to present information.
1.2.2. Effectively present and communicate data through graphs and charts.
1.2.3. Extract, interpret, analyse and evaluate information.
1.2.4. Graphs and charts to be studied: Bar graphs (horizontal, vertical and divided), Histograms (with equal class interval), Line graphs, Scatter graphs (including best fit line), Dispersion graphs, Pie charts, Climate graphs, Proportional symbols, Pictograms, Cross-sections, Population pyramids, Radial graphs, Rose charts.

1.3. Numerical and Statistical Skills
1.3.1. Demonstrate an understanding of number, area and scale.
1.3.2. Demonstrate an understanding of the quantitative relationships between units.
1.3.3. Understand and correctly use proportion, ratio, magnitude and frequency.
1.3.4. Understand and correctly use appropriate measures of central tendency, spread and cumulative frequency including, median, mean, range, quartiles and inter-quartile range, mode and modal class.
1.3.5. Calculate and understand percentages (increase and decrease) and percentiles.
1.3.6. Design fieldwork data collection sheets and collect data with an understanding of accuracy, sample size and procedures, control groups and reliability.
1.3.7. Interpret tables of data.
1.3.8. Describe relationships in bivariate data.
1.3.9. Sketch trend lines through scatter plots.
1.3.10. Draw estimated lines of best fit.
1.3.11. Make predictions; interpolate and extrapolate trends from data.
1.3.12. Be able to identify weaknesses in statistical presentations of data.
1.3.13. Draw and justify conclusions from numerical and statistical data.

1.4. Visual and Written Source Skills
1.4.1. Deconstruct, interpret, analyse and evaluate visual images including photographs, cartoons, pictures and diagrams.
1.4.2. Analyse written articles from a variety of sources for understanding, interpretation and recognition of bias.
1.4.3. Suggest improvements to, issues with or reasons for using maps, graphs, statistical techniques and visual sources, such as photographs and diagrams.

1.5. Fieldwork Skills
1.5.1. Understanding of the kinds of question capable of being investigated through fieldwork and an understanding of the geographical enquiry processes appropriate to investigate these.
1.5.2. Understanding of the range of techniques and methods used in fieldwork, including observation and different kinds of measurement.
1.5.3. Processing and presenting fieldwork data in various ways including maps, graphs and diagrams.
1.5.4. Analysing and explaining data collected in the field using knowledge of relevant geographical case studies and theories.
1.5.5. Drawing evidenced conclusions and summaries from fieldwork transcripts and data.
1.5.6. Reflecting critically on fieldwork data, methods used, conclusions drawn and knowledge gained.
