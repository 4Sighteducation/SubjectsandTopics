1 Module 1: Development of practical skills in geology
1.1 Practical skills assessed in a written examination
1.1.1 Planning
1.1.1.1 (a) experimental design, including to solve problems set in a practical context
1.1.1.2 (b) identification of variables that must be controlled, where appropriate
1.1.1.3 (c) evaluation that an experimental method is appropriate to meet the expected outcomes
1.1.2 Implementing
1.1.2.1 (a) how to use a wide range of practical apparatus and techniques correctly
1.1.2.2 (b) appropriate units for measurements
1.1.2.3 (c) presenting observations and data in an appropriate format
1.1.3 Analysis
1.1.3.1 (a) processing, analysing and interpreting qualitative and quantitative experimental results
1.1.3.2 (b) use of appropriate mathematical skills for analysis of quantitative data
1.1.3.3 (c) appropriate use of significant figures
1.1.3.4 (d) plotting and interpreting suitable graphs from experimental results, including:
1.1.3.4.1 (i) selection and labelling of axes with appropriate scales, quantities and units
1.1.3.4.2 (ii) measurement of gradients and intercepts
1.1.4 Evaluation
1.1.4.1 (a) how to evaluate results and draw conclusions
1.1.4.2 (b) the identification of anomalies in experimental measurements
1.1.4.3 (c) the limitations in experimental procedures
1.1.4.4 (d) precision and accuracy of measurements and data, including margins of error, percentage errors and uncertainties in apparatus
1.1.4.5 (e) the refining of experimental design by suggestion of improvements to the procedures and apparatus
1.2 Practical skills assessed in the Practical Endorsement
1.2.1 Practical skills
1.2.1.1 (a) apply investigative approaches and methods to practical work
1.2.1.2 (b) safely and correctly use a range of practical equipment and materials
1.2.1.3 (c) follow written instructions
1.2.1.4 (d) make and record observations/measurements
1.2.1.5 (e) keep appropriate records of experimental activities
1.2.1.6 (f) present information and data in a scientific way
1.2.1.7 (g) use appropriate software and tools to process data, carry out research and report findings
1.2.1.8 (h) use online and offline research skills including websites, textbooks and other printed scientific sources of information
1.2.1.9 (i) correctly cite sources of information
1.2.1.10 (j) use a wide range of experimental and practical instruments, equipment and techniques appropriate to the knowledge and understanding included in the specification
1.2.2 Use of apparatus and techniques
1.2.2.1 (a) location of geological features in the field using traditional navigation and basic field survey skills without the use of GPS
1.2.2.2 (b) identification of geological structures in the field, recording observations as field sketches
1.2.2.3 (c) use of a compass clinometer to measure two- and three-dimensional geological data across a range of scales such as the dip and strike of planar surfaces, or the apparent dip of fold limbs exposed on a hillside or cliff section
1.2.2.4 (d) construction of graphic logs using appropriate scale and symbol sets for unfamiliar geological sequences and exposures
1.2.2.5 (e) use of sampling techniques in fieldwork
1.2.2.6 (f) application of classification systems using distinguishing characteristics to identify unknown minerals and fossils
1.2.2.7 (g) production of annotated scientific drawings of fossils, or small scale features, from hand samples using a light microscope, or hand lens observation
1.2.2.8 (h) production of full rock descriptions of macro and micro features from conserved hand samples and unfamiliar field exposures
1.2.2.9 (i) use of photomicrographs to identify minerals and rock textures
1.2.2.10 (j) use of appropriate apparatus to record a range of quantitative measurements (to include mass, time, volume, temperature and length)
1.2.2.11 (k) use of physical and chemical testing to identify minerals to include:
1.2.2.11.1 (i) density test
1.2.2.11.2 (ii) Mohs hardness test
1.2.2.12 (l) use of methods to increase accuracy of measurements, such as timing over multiple observations, or use of a fiducial scale (in photograph/field sketch)
1.2.2.13 (m) use of ICT to:
1.2.2.13.1 (i) compile and analyse geological data sets to enable visualisation using geographic information system (GIS)
1.2.2.13.2 (ii) collect, process and model geological data
1.3 Practical skills developed through fieldwork and assessed in a written examination
1.3.1 Practical skills developed through fieldwork
1.3.1.1 (a) the measurement and description of the diagnostic properties of rocks in the field
1.3.1.2 (b) the collection of valid data in the field relating to the igneous, metamorphic or sedimentary processes that formed the rocks
1.3.1.3 (c) the measurement and description of rock deformation in the field
1.3.1.4 (d) the use of geochronological principles in the field to place geological events in relative time sequences
