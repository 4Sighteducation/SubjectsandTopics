1. The Making of America, 1789–1900
   1.1. The Early Republic, 1789–1838
       1.1.1. The Constitution and the new government
           1.1.1.1. Learners will understand the creation and significance of the US Constitution.
           1.1.1.2. Learners should explore the challenges faced by the new government.
       1.1.2. Expansion and conflict
           1.1.2.1. Learners will examine the reasons for and impact of territorial expansion.
           1.1.2.2. Learners should analyze the conflicts with Native Americans.
       1.1.3. The growth of democracy
           1.1.3.1. Learners will study the development of political parties.
           1.1.3.2. Learners should understand the changes in voting rights.
   1.2. The West, 1839–1860
       1.2.1. Manifest Destiny and its impact
           1.2.1.1. Learners will explore the concept of Manifest Destiny.
           1.2.1.2. Learners should assess the impact on Native American lands.
       1.2.2. The Gold Rush and economic growth
           1.2.2.1. Learners will understand the causes and effects of the Gold Rush.
           1.2.2.2. Learners should evaluate the economic changes in the West.
       1.2.3. The role of women and families
           1.2.3.1. Learners will study the experiences of women in the West.
           1.2.3.2. Learners should analyze the impact on family life.
   1.3. Civil War and Reconstruction, 1861–1877
       1.3.1. Causes of the Civil War
           1.3.1.1. Learners will examine the economic and social differences between the North and South.
           1.3.1.2. Learners should understand the role of slavery in causing the war.
       1.3.2. Key events and battles
           1.3.2.1. Learners will study major battles and turning points of the Civil War.
           1.3.2.2. Learners should assess the leadership of key figures.
       1.3.3. Reconstruction policies and their impact
           1.3.3.1. Learners will explore the policies implemented during Reconstruction.
           1.3.3.2. Learners should evaluate the successes and failures of Reconstruction.
   1.4. America’s Gilded Age, 1877–1900
       1.4.1. Industrialization and economic change
           1.4.1.1. Learners will understand the growth of industry and its impact on society.
           1.4.1.2. Learners should analyze the rise of big business and labor movements.
       1.4.2. Immigration and urbanization
           1.4.2.1. Learners will study the patterns and effects of immigration.
           1.4.2.2. Learners should assess the challenges of urbanization.
       1.4.3. Social and cultural developments
           1.4.3.1. Learners will explore changes in American culture and society.
           1.4.3.2. Learners should evaluate the impact of technological advancements.
