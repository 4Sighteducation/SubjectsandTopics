1. English Language (J351)
   1.1 Components
      1.1.1 Component 01: Communicating information and ideas
         1.1.1.1 Section A: Reading information and ideas
            1.1.1.1.1 Read unseen non-fiction texts from the 19th century and either the 20th or 21st century.
            1.1.1.1.2 Learners should be able to:
               1.1.1.1.2.1 Identify and interpret key ideas and information from texts
               1.1.1.1.2.2 Comment on writers’ choices of vocabulary, form and grammatical features, paying attention to detail
               1.1.1.1.2.3 Explore the effects of writing for particular audiences and purposes
               1.1.1.1.2.4 Summarise ideas and information from a single text and synthesise from more than one text
               1.1.1.1.2.5 Draw inferences and justify points of view by referring closely to evidence from the text
               1.1.1.1.2.6 Use appropriate linguistic terminology to support their analysis
               1.1.1.1.2.7 Evaluate the usefulness of a text by identifying bias and misuse of evidence
               1.1.1.1.2.8 Use a broad understanding of the text’s context to inform their reading
               1.1.1.1.2.9 Explore connections across texts to develop their understanding of the ideas, attitudes and values presented in them
         1.1.1.2 Section B: Writing for audience, impact and purpose
            1.1.1.2.1 Writing original non-fiction for different audiences and purposes
            1.1.1.2.2 Learners should be able to:
               1.1.1.2.2.1 Organise ideas and information clearly and coherently
               1.1.1.2.2.2 Select and emphasise key ideas and information to influence readers and reflect the purpose of the writing
               1.1.1.2.2.3 Maintain a consistent viewpoint across a non-fiction piece of writing
               1.1.1.2.2.4 Make considered choices of vocabulary and grammar to reflect particular audiences, purposes and contexts
               1.1.1.2.2.5 Adapt tone, style and register as appropriate
               1.1.1.2.2.6 Use the knowledge gained from wider reading of non-fiction to inform language choices and techniques
               1.1.1.2.2.7 Make appropriate use of information provided by others to write in different forms
               1.1.1.2.2.8 Cite evidence and quotation effectively to support views
               1.1.1.2.2.9 Write to create emotional impact
               1.1.1.2.2.10 Use a range of sentence structures for clarity, purpose and effect, with accurate punctuation and spelling
      1.1.2 Component 02: Exploring effects and impact
         1.1.2.1 Section A: Reading meaning and effects
            1.1.2.1.1 Reading unseen prose fiction texts from the 20th and/or 21st century
            1.1.2.1.2 Learners should be able to:
               1.1.2.1.2.1 Comment on writers’ choices of vocabulary, form and grammatical features and how these create meaning
               1.1.2.1.2.2 Analyse and compare writers’ use of language, paying attention to detail
               1.1.2.1.2.3 Draw inferences and justify points of view by referring closely to evidence from the text
               1.1.2.1.2.4 Interpret writers’ meanings and effects in single texts and across two texts
               1.1.2.1.2.5 Identify the main themes and ideas in texts
               1.1.2.1.2.6 Use appropriate linguistic and literary terminology to support their analysis
               1.1.2.1.2.7 Evaluate how form and structure contribute to the effectiveness and impact of a text
               1.1.2.1.2.8 Use a broad understanding of the text’s context to inform their reading
               1.1.2.1.2.9 Explore connections across texts to develop their understanding of the ideas, attitudes and values presented in them
         1.1.2.2 Section B: Writing imaginatively and creatively
            1.1.2.2.1 Producing original creative writing
            1.1.2.2.2 Learners should be able to:
               1.1.2.2.2.1 Organise and structure ideas in narrative writing to create deliberate effects
               1.1.2.2.2.2 Maintain a consistent viewpoint across a piece of writing, making conscious decisions, for example, about narrative point of view
               1.1.2.2.2.3 Make considered choices of vocabulary and grammar to create deliberate effects
               1.1.2.2.2.4 Use the knowledge gained from wide reading of prose fiction and literary non-fiction to inform language choices and techniques
               1.1.2.2.2.5 Use language creatively and imaginatively
               1.1.2.2.2.6 Adapt tone, style and register as appropriate
               1.1.2.2.2.7 Select and emphasise key ideas to create meaning and influence readers
               1.1.2.2.2.8 Use a range of sentence structures for clarity, purpose and effect, with accurate punctuation and spelling
