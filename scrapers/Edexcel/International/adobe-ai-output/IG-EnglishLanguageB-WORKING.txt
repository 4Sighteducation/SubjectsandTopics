1. Component 1: Reading and Writing (examined)
   1.1 Section A: Reading
       1.1.1 Study and analyse selections from a range of texts
           1.1.1.1 Text types include fiction, journalism, speeches, journals, and reference book extracts
           1.1.1.2 Literary non-fiction texts such as autobiography, letters, obituaries, and travel writing
       1.1.2 Skills development
           1.1.2.1 Read substantial pieces of writing
           1.1.2.2 Develop skills of interpretation and analysis
   1.2 Section B: Reading and Writing
       1.2.1 Explore and develop transactional writing skills
           1.2.1.1 Use learned text types to inspire and influence imaginative writing
           1.2.1.2 Practise a range of fiction and non-fiction writing techniques
       1.2.2 Writing task types
           1.2.2.1 Article for a magazine or newspaper
           1.2.2.2 Speech/talk
           1.2.2.3 Letter
           1.2.2.4 Contribution to a website
   1.3 Section C: Writing
       1.3.1 Explore and develop discursive, narrative, and descriptive writing skills
       1.3.2 Writing task from a choice of three (discursive, narrative, and descriptive)

2. Spoken Language endorsement (optional)
   2.1 Presentation skills in a formal setting
       2.1.1 Speech or talk by a student, followed by questions from the audience
       2.1.2 Formal debate or dialogue, such as an interview
   2.2 Audience requirements
       2.2.1 Presentation to an audience including the teacher
       2.2.2 Presentation designed for a potentially wider audience
   2.3 Assessment criteria
       2.3.1 Pass, Merit, Distinction criteria
       2.3.2 General criteria: audible, use of spoken Standard English
       2.3.3 Pass criteria: straightforward ideas, attempt to organise and structure
       2.3.4 Merit criteria: challenging ideas, clear organisation, achieves purpose
       2.3.5 Distinction criteria: sophisticated ideas, effective strategies, perceptive responses
