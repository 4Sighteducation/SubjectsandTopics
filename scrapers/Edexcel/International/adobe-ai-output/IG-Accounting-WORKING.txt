1. Paper 1: Introduction to Bookkeeping and Accounting
   1.1 Topic 1: The accounting environment
       1.1.1 Types of business organisation
           1.1.1.1 Explain the characteristics of public sector organisations, private sector organisations, sole traders, and partnerships.
           1.1.1.2 Explain the connection between stakeholders and a business, identifying the stakeholders’ use of the business’s financial statements.
       1.1.2 Accounting concepts
           1.1.2.1 Understand the significance of the following accounting concepts: consistency, prudence, accruals, materiality, money measurement, business entity.
       1.1.3 Use of technology in accounting
           1.1.3.1 Explain the benefits of using technology within accounting: accounting software, spreadsheets.
           1.1.3.2 Explain the issues regarding the security of data: data loss, access, confidentiality.
           1.1.3.3 Explain the methods of protecting data: hardware, software.
       1.1.4 Professional ethics
           1.1.4.1 Describe the principles of professional ethics.
           1.1.4.2 Apply the principles of professional ethics to accounting roles and functions.
           1.1.4.3 Explain the concept of public interest and how it is applied to accounting roles and functions.
   1.2 Topic 2: Introduction to bookkeeping
       1.2.1 Business documentation
           1.2.1.1 Explain the purpose of business documents.
           1.2.1.2 Prepare the following business documents: purchase order, purchase invoice, sales invoice, credit note, statement of account, remittance advice, petty cash voucher.
       1.2.2 Books of original entry
           1.2.2.1 Explain the purpose of books of original entry.
           1.2.2.2 Prepare the following books of original entry: purchases day book, sales day book, purchases returns book, sales returns book, three-column cash book, petty cash book, the journal.
       1.2.3 Ledger accounting
           1.2.3.1 Explain the purpose of the nominal ledger, receivables ledger, and payables ledger.
           1.2.3.2 Record transactions in ledger accounts using double entry principles.
       1.2.4 Capital expenditure and revenue expenditure
           1.2.4.1 Explain the terms capital expenditure and revenue expenditure.
           1.2.4.2 Explain the importance of the correct treatment of capital expenditure and revenue expenditure.
       1.2.5 Depreciation
           1.2.5.1 Explain the causes of depreciation.
           1.2.5.2 Distinguish between straight line and reducing balance methods of depreciation.
           1.2.5.3 Calculate and record depreciation in the books of account.
           1.2.5.4 Calculate and record profit or loss of disposal of non-current assets.
       1.2.6 Irrecoverable debts
           1.2.6.1 Explain why it is necessary to provide a provision for irrecoverable debts.
           1.2.6.2 Distinguish between an irrecoverable debt and a provision for an irrecoverable debt.
           1.2.6.3 Calculate and record irrecoverable debts and provision for irrecoverable debts in the books of account.
       1.2.7 Other receivables and payables
           1.2.7.1 Explain why it is necessary to account for other receivables and payables.
           1.2.7.2 Distinguish between other receivables and payables.
           1.2.7.3 Calculate and record other receivables and payables in the books of account.
   1.3 Topic 3: Introduction to control processes
       1.3.1 Trial balance
           1.3.1.1 Explain the purpose of a trial balance.
           1.3.1.2 Prepare a trial balance.
           1.3.1.3 Understand the limitations of a trial balance.
       1.3.2 Control accounts
           1.3.2.1 Explain the purpose of control accounts.
           1.3.2.2 Prepare a trade receivables control account and a trade payables control account.
       1.3.3 Correction of errors
           1.3.3.1 Identify and explain errors that do and do not affect the balancing of the trial balance.
           1.3.3.2 Prepare journal entries to correct errors.
           1.3.3.3 Prepare a suspense account.
       1.3.4 Bank reconciliation statements
           1.3.4.1 Explain the purpose of bank reconciliation statements.
           1.3.4.2 Understand the process of bank reconciliation.
           1.3.4.3 Prepare a bank reconciliation statement.

2. Paper 2: Financial Statements
   2.1 Topic 4: The preparation of financial statements
       2.1.1 Financial statements of a sole trader
           2.1.1.1 Prepare income statements to show gross profit and profit for the year.
           2.1.1.2 Prepare statements of financial position to show assets, liabilities, and equity.
       2.1.2 Financial statements of a partnership
           2.1.2.1 Understand the nature of a partnership and the reasons for forming one.
           2.1.2.2 Understand the nature and structure of a limited liability partnership (LLP).
           2.1.2.3 Apply the provisions of Section 24 of the Partnership Act 1890 in relation to partners’ salaries, division of profit or loss, interest on loans, capital, and drawings.
           2.1.2.4 Understand the nature and purpose of an appropriation account.
           2.1.2.5 Prepare income statements and appropriation accounts.
           2.1.2.6 Prepare partners’ current accounts and capital accounts.
           2.1.2.7 Prepare statements of financial position to include partners’ capital balances and current account balances.
       2.1.3 Incomplete records
           2.1.3.1 Calculate the profit for the year by comparing the open and closing equity figures.
           2.1.3.2 Calculate revenue and purchases using appropriate accounting techniques, to include control accounts.
           2.1.3.3 Prepare income statements and statements of financial position from incomplete records and information.
       2.1.4 The calculation and interpretation of accounting ratios
           2.1.4.1 Explain the difference between profitability and liquidity.
           2.1.4.2 Calculate and interpret the following profitability ratios: gross profit percentage, profit for the year as a percentage of revenue, return on capital employed.
           2.1.4.3 Calculate and interpret the following liquidity ratios: current (working capital) ratio, liquid (acid test) ratio.
       2.1.5 Financial statements of a manufacturer
           2.1.5.1 Understand the difference between the inventories of raw materials, work-in-progress, and finished goods.
           2.1.5.2 Prepare manufacturing accounts to show prime cost, production cost, and total cost.
