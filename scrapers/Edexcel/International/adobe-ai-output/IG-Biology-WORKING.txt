1. Paper 1: Biology Paper 1
   1.1 The nature and variety of living organisms
       1.1.1 Characteristics of living organisms
           1.1.1.1 Understand how living organisms share characteristics such as nutrition, respiration, excretion, response, movement, control of internal conditions, reproduction, growth, and development.
       1.1.2 Variety of living organisms
           1.1.2.1 Describe common features of eukaryotic organisms: plants, animals, fungi, and protoctists.
           1.1.2.2 Describe common features of prokaryotic organisms such as bacteria.
           1.1.2.3 Understand the term pathogen and know that pathogens may include fungi, bacteria, protoctists, or viruses.
   1.2 Structure and functions in living organisms
       1.2.1 Level of organisation
           1.2.1.1 Describe the levels of organisation in organisms: organelles, cells, tissues, organs, and systems.
       1.2.2 Cell structure
           1.2.2.1 Describe cell structures, including the nucleus, cytoplasm, cell membrane, cell wall, mitochondria, chloroplasts, ribosomes, and vacuole.
           1.2.2.2 Describe the functions of the nucleus, cytoplasm, cell membrane, cell wall, mitochondria, chloroplasts, ribosomes, and vacuole.
           1.2.2.3 Know the similarities and differences in the structure of plant and animal cells.
           1.2.2.4 Explain the importance of cell differentiation in the development of specialised cells.
           1.2.2.5 Understand the advantages and disadvantages of using stem cells in medicine.
       1.2.3 Biological molecules
           1.2.3.1 Identify the chemical elements present in carbohydrates, proteins, and lipids (fats and oils).
           1.2.3.2 Describe the structure of carbohydrates, proteins, and lipids as large molecules made up from smaller basic units.
           1.2.3.3 Practical: Investigate food samples for the presence of glucose, starch, protein, and fat.
           1.2.3.4 Understand the role of enzymes as biological catalysts in metabolic reactions.
           1.2.3.5 Understand how temperature changes can affect enzyme function, including changes to the shape of the active site.
           1.2.3.6 Practical: Investigate how enzyme activity can be affected by changes in temperature.
           1.2.3.7 Understand how enzyme function can be affected by changes in pH altering the active site.
           1.2.3.8 Practical: Investigate how enzyme activity can be affected by changes in pH.
       1.2.4 Movement of substances into and out of cells
           1.2.4.1 Understand the processes of diffusion, osmosis, and active transport by which substances move into and out of cells.
           1.2.4.2 Understand how factors affect the rate of movement of substances into and out of cells.
           1.2.4.3 Practical: Investigate diffusion and osmosis using living and non-living systems.
       1.2.5 Nutrition
           1.2.5.1 Understand the process of photosynthesis and its importance in the conversion of light energy to chemical energy.
           1.2.5.2 Know the word equation and the balanced chemical symbol equation for photosynthesis.
           1.2.5.3 Understand how varying carbon dioxide concentration, light intensity, and temperature affect the rate of photosynthesis.
           1.2.5.4 Describe the structure of the leaf and explain how it is adapted for photosynthesis.
           1.2.5.5 Understand that plants require mineral ions for growth.
           1.2.5.6 Practical: Investigate photosynthesis, showing the evolution of oxygen from a water plant.
           1.2.5.7 Understand that a balanced diet should include appropriate proportions of carbohydrate, protein, lipid, vitamins, minerals, water, and dietary fibre.
           1.2.5.8 Identify the sources and describe the functions of various nutrients as components of the diet.
           1.2.5.9 Understand how energy requirements vary with activity levels, age, and pregnancy.
           1.2.5.10 Describe the structure and function of the human alimentary canal.
           1.2.5.11 Understand how food is moved through the gut by peristalsis.
           1.2.5.12 Understand the role of digestive enzymes.
           1.2.5.13 Understand that bile is produced by the liver and stored in the gall bladder.
           1.2.5.14 Understand the role of bile in neutralising stomach acid and emulsifying lipids.
           1.2.5.15 Understand how the small intestine is adapted for absorption.
           1.2.5.16 Practical: Investigate the energy content in a food sample.
       1.2.6 Respiration
           1.2.6.1 Understand how the process of respiration produces ATP in living organisms.
           1.2.6.2 Know that ATP provides energy for cells.
           1.2.6.3 Describe the differences between aerobic and anaerobic respiration.
           1.2.6.4 Know the word equation and the balanced chemical symbol equation for aerobic respiration in living organisms.
           1.2.6.5 Know the word equation for anaerobic respiration in plants and in animals.
           1.2.6.6 Practical: Investigate the evolution of carbon dioxide and heat from respiring seeds or other suitable living organisms.
       1.2.7 Gas exchange
           1.2.7.1 Understand the role of diffusion in gas exchange.
           1.2.7.2 Understand gas exchange in relation to respiration and photosynthesis.
           1.2.7.3 Understand how the structure of the leaf is adapted for gas exchange.
           1.2.7.4 Describe the role of stomata in gas exchange.
           1.2.7.5 Understand how respiration continues during the day and night.
           1.2.7.6 Practical: Investigate the effect of light on net gas exchange from a leaf.
           1.2.7.7 Describe the structure of the thorax.
           1.2.7.8 Understand the role of the intercostal muscles and the diaphragm in ventilation.
           1.2.7.9 Explain how alveoli are adapted for gas exchange by diffusion.
           1.2.7.10 Understand the biological consequences of smoking.
           1.2.7.11 Practical: Investigate breathing in humans.
       1.2.8 Transport
           1.2.8.1 Understand why simple, unicellular organisms can rely on diffusion.
           1.2.8.2 Understand the need for a transport system in multicellular organisms.
           1.2.8.3 Describe the role of phloem and xylem in plants.
           1.2.8.4 Understand how water is absorbed by root hair cells.
           1.2.8.5 Understand that transpiration is the evaporation of water from the surface of a plant.
           1.2.8.6 Understand how the rate of transpiration is affected by environmental factors.
           1.2.8.7 Practical: Investigate the role of environmental factors in determining the rate of transpiration.
           1.2.8.8 Describe the composition of the blood.
           1.2.8.9 Understand the role of plasma in the transport of substances.
           1.2.8.10 Understand how adaptations of red blood cells make them suitable for the transport of oxygen.
           1.2.8.11 Understand how the immune system responds to disease.
           1.2.8.12 Understand how vaccination results in the manufacture of memory cells.
           1.2.8.13 Understand how platelets are involved in blood clotting.
           1.2.8.14 Describe the structure of the heart and how it functions.
           1.2.8.15 Explain how the heart rate changes during exercise and under the influence of adrenaline.
           1.2.8.16 Understand how factors may increase the risk of developing coronary heart disease.
           1.2.8.17 Understand how the structure of arteries, veins, and capillaries relate to their function.
           1.2.8.18 Understand the general structure of the circulation system.
       1.2.9 Excretion
           1.2.9.1 Understand the origin of carbon dioxide and oxygen as waste products of metabolism.
           1.2.9.2 Know the excretory products of the lungs, kidneys, and skin.
           1.2.9.3 Understand how the kidney carries out its roles of excretion and osmoregulation.
           1.2.9.4 Describe the structure of the urinary system.
           1.2.9.5 Describe the structure of a nephron.
           1.2.9.6 Describe ultrafiltration in the Bowman's capsule.
           1.2.9.7 Understand how water is reabsorbed into the blood from the collecting duct.
           1.2.9.8 Understand why selective reabsorption of glucose occurs at the proximal convoluted tubule.
           1.2.9.9 Describe the role of ADH in regulating the water content of the blood.
           1.2.9.10 Understand that urine contains water, urea, and ions.
       1.2.10 Co-ordination and response
           1.2.10.1 Understand how organisms are able to respond to changes in their environment.
           1.2.10.2 Understand that homeostasis is the maintenance of a constant internal environment.
           1.2.10.3 Understand that a co-ordinated response requires a stimulus, a receptor, and an effector.
           1.2.10.4 Understand that plants respond to stimuli.
           1.2.10.5 Describe the geotropic and phototropic responses of roots and stems.
           1.2.10.6 Understand the role of auxin in the phototropic response of stems.
           1.2.10.7 Describe how nervous and hormonal communication control responses.
           1.2.10.8 Understand that the central nervous system consists of the brain and spinal cord.
           1.2.10.9 Understand that stimulation of receptors in the sense organs sends electrical impulses.
           1.2.10.10 Understand the role of neurotransmitters at synapses.
           1.2.10.11 Describe the structure and functioning of a simple reflex arc.
           1.2.10.12 Describe the structure and function of the eye as a receptor.
           1.2.10.13 Understand the function of the eye in focusing on near and distant objects.
           1.2.10.14 Describe the role of the skin in temperature regulation.
           1.2.10.15 Understand the sources, roles, and effects of various hormones.

2. Paper 2: Biology Paper 2
   2.1 Reproduction and inheritance
       2.1.1 Reproduction
           2.1.1.1 Understand the differences between sexual and asexual reproduction.
           2.1.1.2 Understand that fertilisation involves the fusion of a male and female gamete.
           2.1.1.3 Describe the structures of an insect-pollinated and a wind-pollinated flower.
           2.1.1.4 Understand that the growth of the pollen tube followed by fertilisation leads to seed and fruit formation.
           2.1.1.5 Practical: Investigate the conditions needed for seed germination.
           2.1.1.6 Understand how germinating seeds utilise food reserves.
           2.1.1.7 Understand that plants can reproduce asexually by natural and artificial methods.
           2.1.1.8 Understand how the structure of the male and female reproductive systems are adapted for their functions.
           2.1.1.9 Understand the roles of oestrogen and progesterone in the menstrual cycle.
           2.1.1.10 Understand the roles of FSH and LH in the menstrual cycle.
           2.1.1.11 Describe the role of the placenta in the nutrition of the developing embryo.
           2.1.1.12 Understand how the developing embryo is protected by amniotic fluid.
           2.1.1.13 Understand the roles of oestrogen and testosterone in the development of secondary sexual characteristics.
       2.1.2 Inheritance
           2.1.2.1 Understand that the genome is the entire DNA of an organism.
           2.1.2.2 Understand that the nucleus of a cell contains chromosomes on which genes are located.
           2.1.2.3 Describe a DNA molecule as two strands coiled to form a double helix.
           2.1.2.4 Understand that an RNA molecule is single stranded.
           2.1.2.5 Describe the stages of protein synthesis.
           2.1.2.6 Understand how genes exist in alternative forms called alleles.
           2.1.2.7 Understand the meaning of the terms: dominant, recessive, homozygous, heterozygous, phenotype, and genotype.
           2.1.2.8 Understand the meaning of the term codominance.
           2.1.2.9 Understand that most phenotypic features are the result of polygenic inheritance.
           2.1.2.10 Describe patterns of monohybrid inheritance using a genetic diagram.
           2.1.2.11 Understand how to interpret family pedigrees.
           2.1.2.12 Predict probabilities of outcomes from monohybrid crosses.
           2.1.2.13 Understand how the sex of a person is controlled by one pair of chromosomes.
           2.1.2.14 Describe the determination of the sex of offspring at fertilisation.
           2.1.2.15 Understand how division of a diploid cell by mitosis produces two cells that contain identical sets of chromosomes.
           2.1.2.16 Understand that mitosis occurs during growth, repair, cloning, and asexual reproduction.
           2.1.2.17 Understand how division of a cell by meiosis produces four cells, each with half the number of chromosomes.
           2.1.2.18 Understand how random fertilisation produces genetic variation of offspring.
           2.1.2.19 Know that in human cells the diploid number of chromosomes is 46 and the haploid number is 23.
           2.1.2.20 Understand that variation within a species can be genetic, environmental, or a combination of both.
           2.1.2.21 Understand that mutation is a rare, random change in genetic material that can be inherited.
           2.1.2.22 Understand how a change in DNA can affect the phenotype.
           2.1.2.23 Understand how most genetic mutations have no effect on the phenotype.
           2.1.2.24 Understand that the incidence of mutations can be increased by exposure to ionising radiation and some chemical mutagens.
           2.1.2.25 Explain Darwinâ€™s theory of evolution by natural selection.
           2.1.2.26 Understand how resistance to antibiotics can increase in bacterial populations.
