1. Component 1: Non-fiction Texts and Transactional Writing (examined)
   1.1 Section A: Non-fiction texts
       1.1.1 Study and analyse selections from a range of non-fiction texts.
       1.1.2 Read a variety of high-quality, challenging non-fiction texts.
       1.1.3 Respond to one unseen non-fiction text in the examination.
       1.1.4 Develop skills of interpretation and analysis.
       1.1.5 Text types include journalism, speeches, journals, reference book extracts, literary non-fiction.
   1.2 Section B: Transactional Writing
       1.2.1 Explore and develop transactional writing skills.
       1.2.2 Develop non-fiction writing techniques, planning, and proofreading skills.
       1.2.3 Transactional text types: article, speech, letter, guide, review, text of a leaflet.

2. Component 2: Poetry and Prose Texts and Imaginative Writing (examined)
   2.1 Section A: Poetry and Prose Texts
       2.1.1 Study and analyse selections from a range of fictional poetry and prose texts.
       2.1.2 Read substantial pieces of writing that make significant demands in terms of content, structure, and language.
       2.1.3 Develop skills of inference and analysis.
   2.2 Section B: Imaginative Writing
       2.2.1 Explore and develop imaginative writing skills.
       2.2.2 Use what is learned about the writer’s craft to inspire and influence imaginative writing.
       2.2.3 Develop creative writing techniques, planning, and proofreading skills.

3. Component 3: Poetry and Prose Texts and Imaginative Writing (non-examined assessment)
   3.1 Assignment A: Poetry and Prose Texts
       3.1.1 Study and analyse selections from a range of fictional poetry and prose texts.
       3.1.2 Read substantial pieces of writing that make significant demands in terms of content, structure, and language.
       3.1.3 Develop skills of inference and analysis.
   3.2 Assignment B: Imaginative Writing
       3.2.1 Explore and develop imaginative writing skills.
       3.2.2 Use what is learned about the writer’s craft to inspire and influence imaginative writing.
       3.2.3 Develop creative writing techniques, planning, and proofreading skills.

4. Spoken Language Endorsement (optional)
   4.1 Presentation skills in a formal setting.
   4.2 Listen and respond to questions and feedback.
   4.3 Use spoken English effectively.
   4.4 Presentation may take a variety of forms, including a speech or talk, formal debate, or dialogue.
