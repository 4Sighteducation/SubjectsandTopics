1. Chemistry Paper 1
   1.1 Principles of chemistry
       1.1.1 States of matter
           1.1.1.1 Understand the three states of matter in terms of the arrangement, movement and energy of the particles
           1.1.1.2 Understand the interconversions between the three states of matter
       1.1.2 Elements, compounds and mixtures
           1.1.2.1 Classify a substance as an element, compound or mixture
           1.1.2.2 Understand that a pure substance has a fixed melting and boiling point
       1.1.3 Atomic structure
           1.1.3.1 Know the structure of an atom in terms of the positions, relative masses and relative charges of sub-atomic particles
           1.1.3.2 Know what is meant by the terms atomic number, mass number, isotopes and relative atomic mass (Ar)
       1.1.4 The Periodic Table
           1.1.4.1 Understand how elements are arranged in the Periodic Table
           1.1.4.2 Understand how to deduce the electronic configurations of the first 20 elements
       1.1.5 Chemical formulae, equations and calculations
           1.1.5.1 Write word equations and balanced chemical equations
           1.1.5.2 Calculate relative formula masses (including relative molecular masses) (Mr) from relative atomic masses (Ar)
       1.1.6 Ionic bonding
           1.1.6.1 Understand how ions are formed by electron loss or gain
           1.1.6.2 Understand ionic bonding in terms of electrostatic attractions
       1.1.7 Covalent bonding
           1.1.7.1 Know that a covalent bond is formed between atoms by the sharing of a pair of electrons
           1.1.7.2 Understand covalent bonds in terms of electrostatic attractions
       1.1.8 Metallic bonding
           1.1.8.1 Know how to represent a metallic lattice by a 2-D diagram
           1.1.8.2 Understand metallic bonding in terms of electrostatic attractions
       1.1.9 Electrolysis
           1.1.9.1 Understand why covalent compounds do not conduct electricity
           1.1.9.2 Understand why ionic compounds conduct electricity only when molten or in aqueous solution

   1.2 Inorganic chemistry
       1.2.1 Group 1 (alkali metals) – lithium, sodium and potassium
           1.2.1.1 Understand how the similarities in the reactions of these elements with water provide evidence for their recognition as a family of elements
           1.2.1.2 Explain the trend in reactivity in Group 1 in terms of electronic configurations
       1.2.2 Group 7 (halogens) – chlorine, bromine and iodine
           1.2.2.1 Know the colours, physical states (at room temperature) and trends in physical properties of these elements
           1.2.2.2 Explain the trend in reactivity in Group 7 in terms of electronic configurations
       1.2.3 Gases in the atmosphere
           1.2.3.1 Know the approximate percentages by volume of the four most abundant gases in dry air
           1.2.3.2 Understand how to determine the percentage by volume of oxygen in air using experiments
       1.2.4 Reactivity series
           1.2.4.1 Understand how metals can be arranged in a reactivity series based on their reactions
           1.2.4.2 Understand the terms oxidation, reduction, redox, oxidising agent, reducing agent
       1.2.5 Extraction and uses of metals
           1.2.5.1 Know that most metals are extracted from ores found in the Earth’s crust
           1.2.5.2 Explain the uses of aluminium, copper, iron and steel in terms of their properties
       1.2.6 Acids, alkalis and titrations
           1.2.6.1 Describe the use of litmus, phenolphthalein and methyl orange to distinguish between acidic and alkaline solutions
           1.2.6.2 Know that acids in aqueous solution are a source of hydrogen ions and alkalis in aqueous solution are a source of hydroxide ions
       1.2.7 Acids, bases and salt preparations
           1.2.7.1 Know the general rules for predicting the solubility of ionic compounds in water
           1.2.7.2 Describe an experiment to prepare a pure, dry sample of a soluble salt
       1.2.8 Chemical tests
           1.2.8.1 Describe tests for gases: hydrogen, oxygen, carbon dioxide, ammonia, chlorine
           1.2.8.2 Describe how to carry out a flame test

   1.3 Physical chemistry
       1.3.1 Energetics
           1.3.1.1 Know that chemical reactions in which heat energy is given out are described as exothermic
           1.3.1.2 Calculate the heat energy change from a measured temperature change using the expression Q = mcΔT
       1.3.2 Rates of reaction
           1.3.2.1 Describe experiments to investigate the effects of changes in surface area of a solid, concentration of a solution, temperature and the use of a catalyst on the rate of a reaction
           1.3.2.2 Explain the effects of changes in surface area of a solid, concentration of a solution, pressure of a gas and temperature on the rate of a reaction in terms of particle collision theory
       1.3.3 Reversible reactions and equilibria
           1.3.3.1 Know that some reactions are reversible and this is indicated by the symbol in equations
           1.3.3.2 Know that a reversible reaction can reach dynamic equilibrium in a sealed container

   1.4 Organic chemistry
       1.4.1 Introduction
           1.4.1.1 Know that a hydrocarbon is a compound of hydrogen and carbon only
           1.4.1.2 Understand how to represent organic molecules using empirical formulae, molecular formulae, general formulae, structural formulae and displayed formulae
       1.4.2 Crude oil
           1.4.2.1 Know that crude oil is a mixture of hydrocarbons
           1.4.2.2 Describe how the industrial process of fractional distillation separates crude oil into fractions
       1.4.3 Alkanes
           1.4.3.1 Know the general formula for alkanes
           1.4.3.2 Explain why alkanes are classified as saturated hydrocarbons
       1.4.4 Alkenes
           1.4.4.1 Know that alkenes contain the functional group >C=C<
           1.4.4.2 Explain why alkenes are classified as unsaturated hydrocarbons
       1.4.5 Alcohols
           1.4.5.1 Know that alcohols contain the functional group −OH
           1.4.5.2 Know that ethanol can be oxidised by burning in air or oxygen
       1.4.6 Carboxylic acids
           1.4.6.1 Know that carboxylic acids contain the functional group
           1.4.6.2 Describe the reactions of aqueous solutions of carboxylic acids with metals and metal carbonates
       1.4.7 Esters
           1.4.7.1 Know that esters contain the functional group
           1.4.7.2 Know that ethyl ethanoate is the ester produced when ethanol and ethanoic acid react in the presence of an acid catalyst
       1.4.8 Synthetic polymers
           1.4.8.1 Know that an addition polymer is formed by joining up many small molecules called monomers
           1.4.8.2 Explain problems in the disposal of addition polymers
