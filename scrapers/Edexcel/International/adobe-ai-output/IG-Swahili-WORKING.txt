1. Paper 1: Reading, Writing and Translation
   1.1 Content description
       1.1.1 Reading
           1.1.1.1 Students must be able to read and respond to material from a variety of sources.
           1.1.1.2 Demonstrate their ability to summarise a short text by writing the main points clearly.
           1.1.1.3 Interpret and infer implicit meaning, including writers’ thoughts, feelings and ideas.
       1.1.2 Writing
           1.1.2.1 Students must be able to write for a variety of audiences, such as friends and acquaintances, teachers, family members, groups and professional bodies.
           1.1.2.2 Write using a wide range of grammatical forms and structures.
           1.1.2.3 Write using a wide range of relevant and appropriate vocabulary.
           1.1.2.4 Use accurate spelling.
           1.1.2.5 Structure their work effectively.
       1.1.3 Translation
           1.1.3.1 Students are required to convey their understanding of written English and translate it into Swahili to demonstrate an ability to transfer meaning accurately into Swahili.
           1.1.3.2 Apply the conventions of grammar in order to produce meaningful and accurate content.
       1.1.4 Vocabulary
           1.1.4.1 Teachers should present and exploit a range of vocabulary relevant to each theme listed in Appendix 7.
   1.2 Assessment information
       1.2.1 This paper assesses reading, writing and translation skills.
       1.2.2 There are three sections in the paper.
           1.2.2.1 Section A: Reading
               1.2.2.1.1 Questions will test the following assessment objective: AO1 - Understand and respond, in writing, to written language.
           1.2.2.2 Section B: Writing
               1.2.2.2.1 Questions will test the following assessment objective: AO2 - Communicate in writing, using a register appropriate to the situation, showing knowledge of a range of vocabulary in common usage and of the grammar and structures prescribed in the specification, and using them accurately.
           1.2.2.3 Section C: Translation
               1.2.2.3.1 Questions will test the following assessment objective: AO3 - Understand written sentences in English and translate them into Swahili.

2. Paper 2: Listening
   2.1 Content description
       2.1.1 Listening
           2.1.1.1 Students must be able to identify and note main points.
           2.1.1.2 Deduce the meaning of words from context.
           2.1.1.3 Extract specific details.
           2.1.1.4 Identify points of view.
           2.1.1.5 Show some understanding of unfamiliar language.
           2.1.1.6 Recognise attitudes, emotions and opinions.
   2.2 Assessment information
       2.2.1 This paper assesses listening skills.
       2.2.2 Students must answer all questions.
       2.2.3 Questions are set on any of the five themes.
       2.2.4 The paper will include multiple-choice and short-response questions.
       2.2.5 The assessment is 30 minutes (plus 5 minutes’ reading time).
       2.2.6 The paper is worth 40 marks.
       2.2.7 Questions will test the following assessment objective: AO4 - Understand and respond, in writing, to spoken language.

3. Spoken language endorsement (optional)
   3.1 Overview
       3.1.1 The spoken languages endorsement is an optional endorsement available alongside the Pearson Edexcel International GCSE in Swahili qualification.
       3.1.2 If a student completes this endorsement, the endorsement will appear on their certificate as a separate reported grade (pass, merit, distinction or not classified) alongside the overall grade issued for the Pearson Edexcel International GCSE in Swahili qualification (based on papers 1 and 2).
   3.2 Content description
       3.2.1 Spoken language
           3.2.1.1 Students are required to demonstrate their use of spoken Swahili through a presentation based on a topic of their choice.
           3.2.1.2 Students must be able to convey information in speech on a range of topics, using context-specific vocabulary.
           3.2.1.3 Express their opinions about a topic, developing their ideas through speech.
           3.2.1.4 Respond spontaneously to both predictable and unpredictable questions on a range of topics.
           3.2.1.5 Speak fluently and coherently, using a range of grammatical structures without undue hesitation or obvious searching for vocabulary.
   3.3 Assessment information
       3.3.1 There will be a presentation by the student, which will be marked by the centre and monitored by Pearson.
       3.3.2 The presentation should last no longer than 10 minutes.
       3.3.3 All students work should be marked in time for Pearson to monitor the marking. This will need to be completed in the year of certification.
       3.3.4 Tasks will test the following assessment objective: AO5 - Demonstrate presentation skills in a formal setting, listen and respond appropriately to spoken language, including to questions and feedback to presentations, use spoken Swahili effectively in speeches and presentations.
