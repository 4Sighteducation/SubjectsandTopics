1. Paper 1: Reading, Summary and Grammar
   1.1 Content description
       1.1.1 Reading
           1.1.1.1 Read and respond to material from a variety of sources.
           1.1.1.2 Scan for specific information.
           1.1.1.3 Interpret and infer explicit and implicit meaning, including writers’ thoughts, feelings, and ideas.
           1.1.1.4 Understand and analyse how writers create effect.
           1.1.1.5 Select and use relevant evidence from the text to support their answer.
           1.1.1.6 Make comparisons between texts.
       1.1.2 Summary
           1.1.2.1 Demonstrate the ability to summarise a short text by writing the main points clearly.
       1.1.3 Grammar
           1.1.3.1 Develop awareness of the rules of grammar to understand relationships between parts of speech in a text.
           1.1.3.2 Apply the conventions of grammar to produce and manipulate pertinent and accurate content.
   1.2 Assessment information
       1.2.1 Reading
           1.2.1.1 Short- and long-answer questions related to two text extracts.
           1.2.1.2 Total of 49 marks for this section.
       1.2.2 Summary
           1.2.2.1 A 6-mark summary question.
       1.2.3 Grammar
           1.2.3.1 Two 10-mark questions, each assessing different grammar skills.
   1.3 Assessment criteria
       1.3.1 The writer’s craft mark grid
       1.3.2 Comparative response mark grid

2. Paper 2: Writing
   2.1 Content description
       2.1.1 Writing
           2.1.1.1 Communicate meaning effectively in written language in a variety of forms, genres, and styles.
           2.1.1.2 Focus writing on a particular purpose and audience.
           2.1.1.3 Write imaginatively, using a variety of techniques.
           2.1.1.4 Use a wide range of suitable vocabulary.
           2.1.1.5 Use accurate spelling.
           2.1.1.6 Employ a variety of sentence structures for effect.
           2.1.1.7 Structure their work effectively.
           2.1.1.8 Use complex structures.
           2.1.1.9 Use the rules of grammar to create effect, variety, and interest.
   2.2 Assessment information
       2.2.1 Writing
           2.2.1.1 Two 25-mark extended-writing questions out of a choice of three.
   2.3 Assessment criteria
       2.3.1 Communication and content mark grid
       2.3.2 Application and accuracy of language mark grid
