1. Component 1: Poetry and Modern Prose (examined)
   1.1 Unseen Poetry
       1.1.1 Explore how writers create meaning and effects in unseen poetry.
       1.1.2 Demonstrate understanding of poetic form, content, and meaning.
       1.1.3 Use appropriate literary terminology.
   1.2 Anthology Poetry
       1.2.1 Compare a range of poetry from Part 3 of the Pearson Edexcel International GCSE English Anthology.
       1.2.2 Demonstrate understanding of how writers create literary effects.
       1.2.3 Use appropriate literary terminology.
   1.3 Modern Prose
       1.3.1 Explore how writers create meaning and effects in modern prose texts.
       1.3.2 Demonstrate a close understanding of the prose text.
       1.3.3 Understand the relationship between the prose text and its context.

2. Component 2: Modern Drama and Literary Heritage Texts (examined)
   2.1 Modern Drama
       2.1.1 Demonstrate knowledge and understanding of the drama text.
       2.1.2 Understand how writers use language, form, and structure to create effects.
       2.1.3 Use appropriate literary terminology.
   2.2 Literary Heritage Texts
       2.2.1 Demonstrate knowledge and understanding of the literary heritage text.
       2.2.2 Understand the relationship between the text and its context.
       2.2.3 Use appropriate literary terminology.

3. Component 3: Modern Drama and Literary Heritage Texts (non-examined)
   3.1 Assignment A – Modern Drama
       3.1.1 Respond to a teacher-devised assignment on a modern drama text.
       3.1.2 Demonstrate knowledge and understanding of the drama text.
       3.1.3 Use appropriate literary terminology.
   3.2 Assignment B – Literary Heritage Texts
       3.2.1 Respond to a teacher-devised assignment on a literary heritage text.
       3.2.2 Demonstrate knowledge and understanding of the literary heritage text.
       3.2.3 Understand the relationship between the text and its context.
